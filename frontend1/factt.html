Why Donate Blood?

Every two seconds, someone needs blood. By donating blood, you can help save lives and contribute to a healthier community. Blood donation is a simple act that makes a significant impact.

Facts About Blood Donation

  • One donation can save up to three lives.
  • There is no substitute for human blood.
  • Healthy donors can donate blood every 56 days.
  • O-negative blood is universal and can be used for any patient.

    • Pre-Donation Screening

    Before donating, donors undergo a quick health screening to ensure they are eligible to donate. This includes:

  • Checking blood pressure, pulse, and temperature.
  • Hemoglobin level test to confirm you are not anemic.
  • Medical questionnaire to identify potential risks.

    • Post-Donation Tips

    After donating blood, follow these tips to ensure a smooth recovery:

  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of fluids.
  • Avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activity for 24 hours.
  • Have a healthy snack to restore your energy levels.
  • Report any dizziness or discomfort to staff immediately.

    • Benefits of Blood Donation

  • Helps save lives in emergencies and surgeries.
  • Stimulates the production of new blood cells.
  • Improves heart health by reducing excess iron levels.
  • Provides a sense of community and purpose.
